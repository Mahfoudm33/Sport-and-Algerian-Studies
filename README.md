### Project Lead: Prof. Mahfoud Amara (Associate Professor, Qatar University)

[![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-blue.svg)](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)

## 📌 Project Overview
This interactive geospatial platform serves as a digital repository for over two decades of scholarly research regarding the sociopolitical landscape of sport in Algeria. By mapping publications across the North-South axis of the country, this project allows students, academics, and practitioners to explore the evolution of Algerian sport from colonial history to contemporary global mega-events.

The platform is designed for **accessibility**, **geographic context**, and **knowledge dissemination**, providing extensive summaries and theoretical contributions for each listed publication.

## 🗺️ Geospatial & Research Scope
The map covers research nodes from the Mediterranean coast to the Saharan deep south, maintaining a balanced representation of Algeria's territorial and cultural depth. 

**Key geographic anchors include:**
* **Oran:** Focusing on the 19th Mediterranean Games (2022) and the 'New Algeria' narrative.
* **Algiers:** Addressing youth politics, football subcultures, and the transition from socialism to a market economy.
* **Tizi Ouzou:** Researching gender, resistance, and women’s boxing (Imane Khelif & Ghada Mahat).
* **Tamanrasset & The South:** Exploring the Saharan dimension of post-colonial nation-state formation.

*Note: The map utilizes standard OpenStreetMap delineations to ensure accurate representation of international borders, including the Western Sahara boundary.*

## 🧪 Core Research Themes
* **Colonial & Post-Colonial History:** The negotiation of identity and memory through sport.
* **Nation-State Formation:** The role of football in building and legitimizing the sovereign state.
* **Geopolitics & Diplomacy:** Algeria’s strategic positioning within the Mediterranean and Arab world.
* **Gender & Youth:** Resistance, agency, and sub-cultures among women and the younger generation.
* **Economic Transition:** The professionalization of sport and the shift toward corporatized models.

## 🚀 Key Features
- **Interactive Map:** Clickable markers for major cities containing localized research summaries.
- **Contribution to Knowledge:** Each entry includes a specific breakdown of how the research adds to the broader field of North African studies.
- **Keyword Filtering:** Sort publications by themes like *Legitimacy*, *Geopolitics*, or *Gender*.
- **Data Portability:** Students can download the full publication list as a **CSV file** for citation management.
- **Direct Links:** One-click access to full texts via DOI, JSTOR, or MERIP.
